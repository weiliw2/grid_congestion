# BNEF Style Report Template

## Executive Summary

- Which PJM and ERCOT regions show persistent price separation?
- How much of that spread appears to reflect transmission congestion?
- Do high-renewable and low-net-load periods coincide with abnormal spread or negative pricing?
- What does this imply for transmission, storage, and modernization priorities?

## Question 1: Persistent Regional Price Separation

- Where are the most persistent nodal and zonal spreads?
- Which nodes repeatedly rank near the top of congestion exposure?
- How does PJM compare with ERCOT in spread persistence?

## Question 2: Price Spread as Congestion Signal

- How closely do large spreads coincide with high congestion components?
- Where does spread appear to be a strong congestion proxy?
- Where should spread be interpreted more cautiously?

## Question 3: Renewable Output, Net Load, and Abnormal Pricing

- During high-renewable periods, does congestion intensify or ease?
- During low-net-load periods, do negative prices become more frequent?
- Which regions appear structurally sensitive to renewable buildout?

## Question 4: Investment and Modernization Implications

- Which patterns suggest a transmission bottleneck worth upgrading?
- Where could storage help absorb oversupply or reduce spread volatility?
- What broader modernization priorities emerge across the two markets?

## Methodology

- Raw market data sources
- Cleaning and harmonization rules
- Congestion and spread metrics
- Limits of interpreting LMP spread as a congestion proxy
